If you're going to Hamburg, here are some things you might want to see and/or do.
Not necessarily super secret insider tips, but still, maybe it saves you some googling and searching or inspires you in some way.

# Daytime

## Alter Elbtunnel
Walk under the river Elbe and have a look at the city from the south bank of the river. Must do, don’t miss it. Don’t take the stairs or the pedestrian elevator, instead get into one of the open car elevator cabins with the cars and cyclists and enjoy the view of the machinery in operation.   
 [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elbe_Tunnel_(1911)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elbe_Tunnel_(1911))


## Take a harbour ferry
The "HADAG" ferries are part of the public transport network, you can use them with a regular subway ticket, day pass etc. 
Good starting points are Elbphilharmonie, Landungsbrücken, Teufelsbrück   
[https://hadag.de/en/routes/](https://hadag.de/en/routes/)


The guided harbour tours can also be fun, though. There should be ones with English guides. Choose a small “barkasse” over the big white ships you’ll also see (there are actually some canals and bridges where the big ones can’t pass, so insiders go for a barkasse). Cost is probably around 15,- €/person for roughly 1 hour. They all start somewhere at Landungsbrücken.


## Bicycle tour to Kaltehofe, stop at Entenwerder or/and Café Schlick.
Kaltehofe offers a family-friendly café and the a car-free, wide road to get there by bycicle, which makes it a favorite for little outings on a sunny day. And you can go, too, just get a rental bike from [StadtRad](https://stadtrad.hamburg.de/en).   
No matter if you go all the way or only the small part to the Entenwerder Park, make sure to visit the [Entenwerder 1 Café](https://www.facebook.com/entenwerder1/). 
 Another stop for a coffee, but not nearly as glamorous, can be found further down the way if you leave the main track and go to the Moorfleeter Hafen [(Café Schlick)](https://www.facebook.com/pages/category/Cafe/Cafe-Schlick-107037827374894/)


I tried to draw [a possible route here](https://goo.gl/maps/nGWsU89aAQGTVGPy7), but that's more for an overall idea and not necessarily the exact route I've taken in the past. Especially in the western part there's also an [alternative bikepath](https://www.komoot.com/highlight/167318). Best do your own research and of course pick your own starting point. 



## Canoo tour on the Alster, get a coffee
Go to [Zur Gondel](https://www.bootsvermietung-dornheim.de/bootsvermietung) to rent a canoo and paddle out on the Alster and to the Stadtpark. While your at it, why not go past the [Café Canale](https://www.cafecanale.de/)? They serve boats directly on the water, no need to get out to get a coffee.

## Speicherstadt & HafenCity
Go see the model in the [Kesselhaus](https://www.hafencity.com/en/infocenter/kesselhaus) to learn about Germany’s biggest urban development project, visit the [Elbphilharmonie Plaza](https://www.elbphilharmonie.de/en/plaza), and stroll around in the quarter to see both the old storage houses and contemporary architecture.


## Elbstrand

The bank of the river Elbe in Hamburg is long, and from the city center all the way to Hamburg's far Western end there are walkways along it. A particularly nice part is the beach. Not much of a beach really, but still a magic place, with a great atmosphere and wonderful view on the big container terminals. Great during the daytime, but also in the "blue hour" when the lights go on in the harbour. Watch the van carriers buzzing between the containers.

A popular starting place is Museumshafen Övelgönne, just walk West from there. Or you could go to S-Bahn Blankenese, walk down to the river from there and walk East. Or.. well, whatever works for you.

Some highlights:

* [Strandperle](http://www.strandperle-hamburg.de/home.html) & [Strandkiosk](https://www.hamburg.de/bars-kneipen-hamburg/4025284/ahoi-strandkiosk/)
* [Jenisch-Park](https://www.hamburg.de/parkanlagen/3229600/jenischpark/) 
* Blankenese with the Treppenviertel, Strandweg, Lighthouse




# Later in the day, or evening

## Hein-Köllisch-Platz
Great off-Reeperbahn location to have a drink at the [Geyer](https://www.tripadvisor.de/Restaurant_Review-g187331-d1038798-Reviews-Cafe_Geyer-Hamburg.html), especially outdoors in sunny weather. If you walk towards the river from there you'll pass the [Drip Bar](https://www.instagram.com/_dripbar_/), good stop for a drink in the evening.    
[Hein-Köllisch-Platz](https://goo.gl/maps/ACnQSk7MEHkfqARh8)


## Wasserlichtspiele Planten un Blomen

It's so bad it's good again. Music, fountains, coloured lights, the cheesiest thing you can imagine. It probably doesn't attract as many tourists as it used to, but it has a cult following in the city. Some people will bring a plaid and some wine. It's super chill. It's free and in a beautiful park in the city center, so you can't go wrong, just go.   
[Wasserlichtspiele Planten un Blomen](https://www.hamburg-travel.com/see-explore/events/wasserlichtspiele-planten-un-blomen/)


# Later in the evening / at night

## From Schanzenviertel to St. Pauli
The Schanzenviertel is a textbook example for gentrification. The "Rote Flora" remains as a witness of former, alternative lifestyle times. At the turn of the millenium "Die Schanze" was the place to have an office if you wanted to appear creative and innovative, so the ad agencies came and rents skyrocketed. But it's still full of good bars and restaurants. From there, you can walk to the nightlife hub St. Pauli, and will never be more than a hundred meters or so from the next bar, pub, restaurant or club. Go to "Beim Grünen Jäger" and go exploring (by the way the [Nil](https://restaurant-nil.de) there is a great place for dinner). Moving further, follow Wohlwillstraße. Insider Tip: At the end of Wohlwillstraße, don't just cross and move on to Reeperbahn, instead take a right and follow Paul-Roosen-Straße. Pass the Edeka and find the latest generation of Hamburg Bars there. And from there it's just a few steps to the sizzling nightlife of Große Freiheit, Hamburger Berg, Hans-Albers-Platz.   

## Frau Hedi
Party on a boat. Get an awesome harbour tour and shake your booty (or enjoy some chill out beats, depending on the DJ of the evening) at the same time. The boats have a fixed spot where they land hourly to get on or off. One of the most “Hamburg” things you can do.   
[https://frauhedi.de](https://frauhedi.de)


## Have a drink at the "20 up" bar
You may not like the crowd in this Hotel bar, which consists mostly of show-offs, but the location on the 20th floor of the Empire Riverside hotel is unbeatable. Arguably the best harbour view in Hamburg. To see St. Pauli from above, use the men's room.   
[https://www.empire-riverside.de/en/restaurant-and-bars/skyline-bar-20up/](https://www.empire-riverside.de/en/restaurant-and-bars/skyline-bar-20up/)


## Thai Oase
*The* Karaoke bar. Come late night, it will be crowded, drunk, everyone will be singing, it’s unreal. Write down your song title on a piece of paper, hand it to the MC, wait until the song is called, hurry to the stage, and sing your heart out.  
[http://thai-oase.com](http://thai-oase.com)


## Fish Market
A major tourist attraction with market people yelling their offers and throwing samples into the crowd. Sundays 5:30am - 9am. Get up early.. or stay up late?

[https://www.hamburg.de/fischmarkt/](https://www.hamburg.de/fischmarkt/)




# Food
Like any big city Hamburg has a huge variety of restaurants. Probably best check Trip Advisor, where you can filter for all your preferences. For the better restaurants you’ll probably need to make a reservation. At least on Friday night, that seems to be the “eating out” night.

Some typical Hamburg experiences:


## Kleine Brunnenstraße
Really good food, cozy restaurant, nice neighborhood. Currently the favorite in my circle. Upper-middle priced. Some outdoor seating available, for a table inside reservation is advised.

[https://www.kleine-brunnenstrasse.de/](https://www.kleine-brunnenstrasse.de/)



## Cuneo
Old Italian restaurant, right in the middle of St. Pauli. A local treasure, it was the first Italian restaurant in Hamburg. Make a reservation or take your chance at 10pm when the early diners’ tables become available again.
[https://www.cuneo1905.de/](https://www.cuneo1905.de/)


## O Frango
Explore the "Portuguese Quarter". This part of the “Neustadt”, right below St. Pauli, is full of Portuguese joints, all with outside seating, offering a vibrant street life (if the weather is nice). Have some garlicky grilled fish (which is usually a whole fish with fishbones and all - ask the waiter which fish is served as a fillet if you don’t like the fiddling), or get the Piri Piri Chicken in my local favorite O Frango.
[https://www.hamburg-tourism.de/shoppen-geniessen/restaurants-cafes/o-frango/](https://www.hamburg-tourism.de/shoppen-geniessen/restaurants-cafes/o-frango/)


## Parlament
Very Hanseatic restaurant right underneath the beautiful city hall. Popular lunch place in the city center.
[https://www.parlament-hamburg.de/?lang=en](https://www.parlament-hamburg.de/?lang=en)


# Culture

## Sammlung Falckenberg

Of course there’s the [Kunsthalle](https://www.hamburger-kunsthalle.de) and the [Galerie der Gegenwart (Contemporary Art)](https://www.hamburger-kunsthalle.de/en/contemporary-art), and photography in the [Deichtorhallen](https://www.deichtorhallen.de/en/), and they're all great and well worth a visit. But the real highlight in Hamburg's art gallery offering is the Sammlung Falckenberg. An impressive private collection of art from the 1980’s to today.        
[https://www.sammlung-falckenberg.de/en/](https://www.sammlung-falckenberg.de/en/)


## Elbphilharmonie

By no means an insider tip, as it's probably the most famous building in Hamburg today, but still. People come from all over Germany, and further, just to visit a concert here. If you can get hold of a ticket for a concert in the Grand Hall, go.   
[https://www.elbphilharmonie.de/en/](https://www.elbphilharmonie.de/en/)
